Galileo Ways of Work

A socio technical continuous flow model where Discovery, Delivery, and Operations move in parallel, share information continuously, and adapt in real time to uncertainty.

music_note Executive metaphor – dance, not phases

Working at Galileo is closer to dancing with the customer problem than following a rigid choreography. We do not work in a sequence like first listen, then learn the rhythm, then try to dance. Discovery, Delivery, and Operations must hear the music at the same time, react to each other, and adjust in short loops. That is how we reduce residual uncertainty and build resilient flows for e commerce, stores, lab and logistics in Mexico, Chile, and Colombia.

explore Discovery

Clarify problem and context

Discovery reduces uncertainty enough so that a decision is possible. It does not try to know everything. It clarifies the problem, constraints, and impacts with real context from stores, lab and customers.

  • • Archetypes, personas, Jobs to Be Done, context of use
  • • Explicit assumptions and hypotheses
  • • Problem framing, opportunity and impact
  • • Domain modeling: EventStorming, Domain Storytelling
  • • Early feasibility and viability checks
  • • Draft SLIs and SLOs for what success means

Feeds Delivery with clarified scope and constraints. Receives operational reality from Operations to refine assumptions.

code Delivery

Build operable solutions

Delivery creates solutions that are ready to live in production. It balances speed, safety, and technical coherence for Odoo, PoS, lab, logistics and data.

  • • Delivery modes: greenfield, brownfield, strangler, firefighting
  • • Threat modeling, spikes, PoCs
  • • Sacrificial architecture when learning is the main goal
  • • Architecture coherence and Well Architected principles
  • • Fitness functions and living documentation
  • • Observability instrumentation and quality gates

Works with Discovery to adjust feasibility and constraints. Works with Operations to protect error budgets and resilience.

monitor_heart Operations

Prove value in reality

Operations is where the system meets customers, stores and lab. It reveals what really happens: value, bottlenecks, and fragility.

  • • Error budgets, SLI and SLO tracking
  • • Incident detection, triage and resolution
  • • User behavior and operational load tracking
  • • A B testing and impact measurement
  • • Data quality, contracts and lifecycle
  • • Resilience testing and failure analysis

Feeds Discovery with new insights and constraints. Feeds Delivery with performance, reliability and regression patterns.

Product Operative Model

A continuous discovery, delivery, and operations system that connects teams, architecture, and data to the same customer value streams in Mexico, Chile and Colombia.

Executive summary

The Galileo Product Operative Model is a non linear operating system. Stream aligned teams own a bounded context end to end. Platform teams reduce cognitive and operational load with paved roads. Enabling teams uplift capabilities. Complicated subsystem teams manage high complexity areas like Odoo and lab systems. Discovery, Delivery and Operations are always active, with Residuality Theory guiding where to focus, where to invest, and how to reduce residual risk for e commerce, PoS, lab and logistics.

diversity_3 Socio technical architecture

Galileo Platform is a socio technical system. The software architecture and the organization mirror each other. We use stream aligned, platform, enabling and complicated subsystem teams to match bounded contexts, domains and capabilities.

Stream aligned teams

Own a bounded context end to end. They span Discovery, Delivery and Operations for contexts like CommerceFlow, VisionProfile, CustomerCare and lab services.

Platform teams

Technical Platform and Data Platform teams provide infrastructure, identity, observability, data contracts and DevEx paved roads as a service.

Enabling teams

Design and UX enablement, Delivery and Quality enablement teams raise capabilities in product discovery, UX, testing and modern delivery practices. They coach, they do not own flow.

Complicated subsystem teams

Teams dedicated to Odoo core, lab systems and some PoS or innovation subsystems where complexity is high. They interact with stream aligned teams by collaboration or service agreements.

Socio-Technical Architecture

The organizational structure and software architecture of Galileo Platform are one system. Five layers, four team types, continuous feedback loops, and Residuality Theory guide how we build and operate across Mexico, Chile, and Colombia.

architecture Conway's Law, inverted

We do not let the architecture emerge from random org structures. We deliberately design bounded contexts and team boundaries together so that communication flows match value flows. Stream-aligned teams own end-to-end outcomes. Platform and enabling teams reduce their cognitive load.

Layer Details & Team Responsibilities

L5

Core Business

Stream-aligned teams own complete value streams. They run Discovery, Delivery, and Operations for their bounded context. Direct accountability for customer outcomes.

Contexts: Commerce, Vision, Fashion, Care
Team type: Stream-Aligned
L4

Transversal Services

Shared operational capabilities that serve multiple value streams. Mix of stream-aligned and complicated subsystem teams for high-complexity domains like manufacturing and Odoo.

Contexts: Inventory, MfgOps, Logistics, Finance
Team type: Stream-Aligned + Complicated Subsystem
L3

Vertical Enabling

Enabling teams raise capabilities across all other teams. They coach, transfer knowledge, and exit. No production ownership — their success is measured by independence of other teams.

Contexts: Design, Quality, Architecture
Interaction: Facilitation mode
L2

Data Platform

Platform team providing data infrastructure as a service. Data contracts, lineage, quality gates, and governance. Stream-aligned teams consume self-service data products.

Contexts: DataAIPlatform, Analytics, ML
Interaction: X-as-a-Service mode
L1

Technical Platform

Foundation layer. Platform team owns infrastructure, identity, security, observability, and CI/CD. Everything is self-service. Paved roads reduce cognitive load for all layers above.

Contexts: DevPlatform, Security, Compliance
Interaction: X-as-a-Service mode
RT

Residuality Theory

Unknown unknowns concentrate at the top (customer-facing). Platform layers dampen uncertainty. Blast radius containment, sacrificial architecture, and fragility signals guide architectural decisions.

Key concepts: Stressors, exposure zones, dampening
Practice: Design for failure, contain blast radius

Team Interaction Modes

How teams collaborate varies by context. We use four modes from Team Topologies, adapted to our DDO continuous flow model.

handshake

Collaboration

High-bandwidth, temporary. Two teams work closely on a shared problem. Used for new domains, complex integrations, or when crossing context boundaries.

Example: Commerce + Inventory during Buen Fin preparation

cloud

X-as-a-Service

Low friction, self-service. Platform provides capabilities that stream-aligned teams consume without needing to understand internals. Clear API contracts.

Example: CI/CD pipelines, data products, observability stack

school

Facilitation

Enabling teams coach and transfer knowledge. The goal is for the receiving team to become independent. Success = the enabling team can exit.

Example: Quality team coaching testing practices

all_inclusive

Continuous Flow

The default mode within a stream-aligned team. Discovery, Delivery, and Operations run in parallel with short feedback loops. No handoffs.

Example: Any SAT running their bounded context
